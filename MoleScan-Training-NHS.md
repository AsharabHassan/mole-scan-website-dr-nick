# MoleScan for NHS & ICBs — Training Document

## What Is MoleScan?

MoleScan is a dermatologist-led skin lesion triage and assessment platform designed for NHS primary care. It is operated by Dermme Health Ltd, a company registered in England and Wales, and is CQC registered.

MoleScan sits between primary care and secondary care in the NHS skin cancer referral pathway. It enables GPs and primary care practitioners to triage skin lesions before making a 2-week-wait referral, with every case reviewed by a UK consultant dermatologist within 24 hours.

MoleScan is not a medical device. It is a clinical workflow and triage platform. It combines AI-assisted image analysis with UK consultant dermatologist review to deliver structured, RAG-rated skin lesion assessment reports. The final clinical decision always rests with the treating clinician.

## The Problem MoleScan Solves for the NHS

### The Dermatology Referral Crisis

NHS dermatology services face sustained, growing pressure from rising 2-week-wait referral volumes. The numbers tell a stark story:

- **95% of urgent skin cancer referrals** through the 2-week-wait pathway are for benign lesions that did not require urgent secondary care attention.
- **6+ weeks** is the average wait for non-urgent dermatology appointments in many areas.
- **Year-on-year increases** in 2WW skin cancer referrals continue to overwhelm dermatology departments.

This creates a cascade of problems. Dermatology departments are overwhelmed with benign cases. Patients with genuinely suspicious lesions face delays. GPs lack the specialist support to confidently manage skin lesions in primary care. The system wastes resources on unnecessary referrals while potentially missing cases that need urgent attention.

### Why This Happens

GPs and primary care practitioners see skin lesions daily. Many do not have specialist dermatology training. When a patient presents with a suspicious mole, the safest option from a medicolegal perspective is to refer through the 2-week-wait pathway. The GP is not confident enough to reassure the patient that the lesion is benign — so they refer. This is clinically understandable but systemically unsustainable.

What GPs need is expert dermatologist support at the point of decision — before the referral is made. That is exactly what MoleScan provides.

### The MoleScan Solution for NHS

MoleScan gives GPs and primary care practitioners a structured pathway to triage skin lesions before referral:

1. The GP captures dermoscopic images of the skin lesion during the consultation.
2. The images and clinical information are uploaded to MoleScan.
3. Every case is reviewed by a UK GP with Special Interest in dermatology (GPwSI) or consultant dermatologist.
4. A structured, RAG-rated report is delivered within 24 hours.
5. The GP uses the report to make an informed decision: reassure the patient, monitor the lesion, or refer urgently with supporting clinical evidence.

Cases assessed as benign can be safely managed in primary care. Suspicious cases are flagged for urgent referral with the dermatologist's assessment attached — giving the receiving secondary care team a head start.

The result: fewer unnecessary 2WW referrals reaching secondary care. More confident GPs. Faster answers for patients. Better resource utilisation across the system.

## How MoleScan Works — The NHS Pathway

### Step 1: Patient Presents to GP
A patient presents to their GP with a skin lesion concern. This may be during a routine appointment, a skin check, or when the patient has noticed a change in a mole.

### Step 2: GP Assesses the Lesion
The GP performs an initial clinical assessment. If the lesion warrants further evaluation but the GP is not confident making a referral decision, they proceed with MoleScan.

### Step 3: Dermoscopic Images Captured
The GP captures dermoscopic images using a dermoscope attachment on their smartphone. They also complete a structured clinical history within the MoleScan platform: lesion location, size, duration, any reported changes, and relevant patient history.

### Step 4: Case Uploaded to MoleScan
The GP submits the case to MoleScan. The upload takes under 2 minutes. All patient data is encrypted and stored within the United Kingdom.

### Step 5: AI-Assisted Triage
MoleScan's clinical triage layer performs AI-assisted analysis, flagging features of concern and providing preliminary risk stratification. This does not replace dermatologist review — it supports it by helping prioritise cases and ensuring consistency.

### Step 6: UK Dermatologist Reviews the Case
Every case is reviewed by a UK-registered GP with Special Interest in dermatology (GPwSI) or a consultant dermatologist. All reviewers hold current GMC registration and, where applicable, specialist registration in dermatology. Human expertise validates every assessment.

### Step 7: Structured Report Delivered Within 24 Hours
The GP receives a comprehensive clinical report within 24 hours containing:
- The dermatologist's clinical assessment
- A RAG-rated outcome (GREEN, AMBER, or RED)
- Clear recommendations for next steps
- A complete audit trail

### Step 8: RAG-Rated Outcomes Guide the Decision
- **GREEN** — Low risk. Reassure the patient. Monitor if appropriate. No 2WW referral needed.
- **AMBER** — Intermediate risk. Review and monitor. Consider referral if the lesion changes or if clinical concern persists.
- **RED** — High risk. Urgent referral recommended. Fast-track the patient through the 2WW pathway with the MoleScan report attached as supporting clinical evidence.

### Step 9: GP Makes an Informed Decision
With the MoleScan report in hand, the GP can:
- **Reassure** the patient with confidence, backed by a dermatologist's opinion.
- **Monitor** the lesion with a structured follow-up plan.
- **Refer urgently** with clinical evidence that gives secondary care a clear picture before the patient arrives.

## Benefits for NHS Commissioners and ICBs

### Reduce 2WW Referral Pressure
MoleScan enables primary care to filter benign lesions before they reach secondary care. By triaging at the GP level with expert dermatologist support, only the cases that genuinely need a 2WW slot reach dermatology departments.

### Cost-Effective Triage
Every unnecessary 2WW referral costs the NHS in secondary care appointments, clinic time, and diagnostic procedures. By identifying benign lesions at primary care level, MoleScan avoids these costs entirely.

### Patient Safety First
Every assessment is reviewed by a UK dermatologist. Urgent cases are flagged for fast-track referral. No melanoma is missed — while benign cases are managed appropriately without overwhelming secondary care.

### Free Up Routine Dermatology Capacity
By reducing the volume of unnecessary urgent referrals, MoleScan helps free up routine dermatology appointment slots for patients who genuinely need face-to-face specialist assessment.

### Reduce Routine Dermatology Waiting Times
By triaging skin lesions effectively at primary care level, MoleScan helps free up routine dermatology appointment slots for patients who genuinely need specialist face-to-face assessment.

### Consistent Clinical Standards
Every MoleScan report follows a standardised format, ensuring consistent triage quality across all participating practices. This is particularly valuable for ICBs managing skin cancer pathways across multiple GP practices with varying levels of dermatology expertise.

## Clinical Governance and Compliance

MoleScan is built to meet the governance and safety standards expected of NHS-facing digital health platforms.

### Clinical Safety
- Aligned with **DCB0129** (manufacturer clinical safety) and **DCB0160** (deploying organisation clinical safety) standards.
- A formal Clinical Safety Case is maintained and reviewed regularly.
- A designated Clinical Safety Officer (CSO) oversees clinical safety, hazard management, and incident reporting.

- Fully compliant with **UK GDPR** and the Data Protection Act 2018.
- Registered with the **Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)**.
- All patient data stored within the **United Kingdom**. No data transferred outside the UK.

### GMC-Registered Clinicians

- Dermatologists hold current specialist registration in dermatology.

## Evidence Base

MoleScan's approach combines two established clinical methodologies with strong evidence bases:

1. **Structured dermoscopic image assessment** — Dermoscopy has been shown to improve diagnostic accuracy for melanoma by 5-30% compared to naked-eye examination alone. It is the standard of care for skin lesion assessment in dermatology.

2. **Expert teledermatology review** — Store-and-forward teledermatology (capturing images and sending them for remote dermatologist review) has extensive published evidence supporting its effectiveness in triaging skin lesions, reducing unnecessary referrals, and maintaining diagnostic accuracy.

MoleScan combines both approaches with an AI-assisted triage layer, creating a more thorough and consistent process than either approach alone.
