# MoleScan for Private Clinics — Training Document

## What Is MoleScan?

MoleScan is a dermatologist-led mole and skin lesion assessment platform built for private clinics and practitioners who are not dermatologists. It is operated by Dermme Health Ltd, a company registered in England and Wales, and is CQC registered.

MoleScan is not a medical device. It is a clinical workflow and triage platform. It combines AI-assisted image analysis with UK consultant dermatologist review to deliver structured skin lesion assessment reports within 24 hours. The final clinical decision always rests with the treating clinician or practitioner.

---

## The Problem MoleScan Solves for Private Clinics

You run a private clinic. Every day, you work on or near your clients' skin. Whether you are an aesthetician injecting Botox, a beauty therapist performing a facial, a podiatrist treating feet, a physiotherapist working on a shoulder, a laser technician removing hair, or a chiropractor working on a back — you encounter moles and skin lesions regularly.

You are not a dermatologist. Dermatology is not your speciality — and it should not be. But when you spot a mole or skin lesion on a client, you face a difficult choice:

1. **Treat over or around it** and accept the risk. If that mole later turns out to be melanoma, you have no documentation showing you acted responsibly.
2. **Cancel or delay the appointment.** This means lost revenue, a frustrated client, and a gap in your schedule.
3. **Tell the client to see their GP.** This sounds responsible, but it means losing the client for weeks while they wait for an NHS appointment. Many clients never follow through. The mole goes unchecked.

None of these options are acceptable. MoleScan provides a fourth option: **get an expert dermatologist opinion within 24 hours.**

The practitioner captures a dermoscopic image of the mole, uploads it to MoleScan, and receives a structured report from a UK consultant dermatologist within 24 hours. The report tells them whether the mole is safe (GREEN), needs monitoring (AMBER), or requires urgent referral (RED).

No guessing. No waiting weeks. No cancelled appointments. No liability.

## Who Uses MoleScan?

MoleScan is built for any private clinic or practitioner who encounters moles and skin lesions but does not have dermatology expertise. This includes:

### Aesthetic Clinics
Botox, dermal fillers, chemical peels, and microneedling often require working directly on skin where moles may be present. A mole sitting where a practitioner needs to inject cannot be safely treated over without knowing it is safe. MoleScan provides a dermatologist's assessment within 24 hours.

### Beauty Therapists
Facials, waxing, microdermabrasion, and threading bring practitioners into close contact with clients' skin. During treatment, a beauty therapist may spot a mole they have not seen before, or one that looks different. MoleScan lets them flag it professionally without needing clinical training. The client sees the therapist cares about their safety.

### Laser and IPL Clinics
Lasering over an unassessed mole is a serious safety risk. Practitioners must avoid moles during laser treatment, but they need to understand whether it is safe. A MoleScan report either clears the mole or flags it for referral, allowing the practitioner to map their treatment area safely.

### Podiatry Clinics
A dark lesion on the foot or under a toenail can look like a bruise, a verruca, or a subungual melanoma. Podiatrists need to rule out the worst before treating. This is particularly important because acral melanoma — melanoma on the extremities — is one of the most commonly missed forms of skin cancer.

### Physiotherapy and Sports Clinics
Physiotherapists, sports therapists, and rehabilitation practitioners work on exposed skin daily. They may notice a mole on a client's back, shoulder, or leg that the client has never seen themselves. MoleScan turns a casual observation into a professional duty of care.

### Private GP Clinics
Private GPs use MoleScan when patients present with suspicious moles. Rather than waiting for an NHS referral pathway, they can submit dermoscopic images and receive a dermatologist-reviewed report within 24 hours — giving patients timely answers.

### Chiropractors and Massage Therapists
Working on exposed skin, particularly on the back, shoulders, and neck, chiropractors and massage therapists regularly notice moles their clients cannot see. Back melanomas are often detected late because people cannot easily see their own backs. Flagging a suspicious mole through MoleScan could make a life-changing difference.

### Tattoo Removal Studios
Tattoo ink can obscure moles or hide changes in pigmentation. Before lasering a tattooed area, any moles within or near the tattoo must be assessed. MoleScan helps tattoo removal practitioners proceed safely.

## How MoleScan Works — The 10-Step Clinic Pathway

### Step 1: Client Attends Your Clinic
A client arrives for their scheduled appointment — Botox, laser, facial, podiatry, physiotherapy, massage, or any other treatment.

### Step 2: Practitioner Spots a Mole or Skin Lesion
During the appointment, you notice a mole or skin lesion on the client. It could be in the treatment area or elsewhere on exposed skin.

### Step 3: You Recommend a MoleScan Assessment
Rather than ignoring the mole, treating over it, or telling the client to see their GP, you recommend a MoleScan assessment. **Pause. Assess. Then treat.**

### Step 4: Capture Dermoscopic Images and Answer Questions
You answer a short set of pre-determined clinical questions (location, size, any changes the client has noticed) and capture dermoscopic images using a dermoscope attachment on your smartphone. This takes under 2 minutes.

### Step 5: Upload to MoleScan
The images and clinical information are uploaded to the MoleScan platform. All data is encrypted and stored securely within the United Kingdom.

### Step 6: Case Prepared for Expert Review
MoleScan's clinical team reviews the case. AI-assisted analysis flags features of concern and provides initial risk stratification to support the reporting dermatologist.

### Step 7: UK Dermatologist Reviews the Case
Every single case is reviewed by a UK GP with Special Interest in dermatology (GPwSI) or a consultant dermatologist. All reviewers are GMC-registered. Human expertise validates every assessment — no case is ever cleared by AI alone.

### Step 8: Report Delivered Within 24 Hours
You receive a comprehensive, structured report within 24 hours. The report includes the dermatologist's assessment, the RAG-rated outcome, and clear next steps.

### Step 9: RAG-Rated Outcome
- **GREEN** — Low risk. Reassure the client. Proceed with treatment if appropriate.
- **AMBER** — Intermediate risk. Monitor the lesion. A possible referral may be recommended.
- **RED** — High risk. Urgent referral recommended. Do not treat in the lesion area.

### Step 10: You Decide What To Do Next
Armed with the MoleScan report, you act with confidence:
- GREEN: Proceed with the planned treatment.
- AMBER: Reassess. Monitor. Schedule a follow-up.
- RED: Refer the client to their GP or specialist.

**Check first. Treat with confidence.**
